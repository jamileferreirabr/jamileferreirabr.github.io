Iana, the story of Isolda and Elias touched me deeply... My name is Jamile. I'm Brazilian, I live in a city called Belo Horizonte. In 2001, I was in a very bad place – I had been raped, I had no one to talk about, I was alone, and my aunt wanted me to take a course at a church she attended... it was a course in "philosophical anthropology". I really like philosophy, and I went. When the professor entered the room, I was taken aback. He wasn't a man of a "good looks", but he was... perfect for me... discreet, serious, glasses :), tall, thin, light-skinned, and with a powerful voice, incredibly intelligent. I spent two months attending the course, keeping some distance, and only showed him that I was reading a book he mentioned in the course, "Phaedrus" by Plato. He smiled, asked me what I thought of the book, and I don't even know what I answered, because his smile and his gaze completely captivated me. In one class, someone played the guitar... and my aunt said that I played and sang too! I went up on stage, very shy, but I sang with all my heart a song called "Força Estranha", (Strang Force) That's why a force compels me to sing That's why this strange force That's why I sing, I can't stop That's why this voice is so powerful https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JizRoArMVoI And I looked at him—and I'll never forget his face—the most beautiful and gentle smile I had ever seen! ... At the very end of the course, I got a huge shock... he came out of the inner area of ​​the hall fully vested: HE WAS A PRIEST. And I really didn't know! My heart stopped, because I'm Catholic, and in my religion, that's something really serious. I didn't even say goodbye properly. I left, and spent the days trying to forget him. I got a job, and put my mind INSIDE my work. A workaholic was literally born. About three months later, my aunt called me saying that Father Eduardo, yes, "my priest", was moving to Madrid, Spain, for a doctorate at the University, and that there would be a small farewell party. Ever since I saw him and started the course, and influenced by his beautiful words about "being enchanted by humanity," I started writing about my sufferings – the rape, a sick mother (schizophrenia), a father who abandoned me, a younger brother I had to take care of even though I wasn't his mother, serious money problems... and I kept writing to Eduardo. When I found out he was a priest, I couldn't write anymore. But since he was leaving Brazil, and his doctoral thesis was about "the human being," I printed out what I had written. It was still incomplete, because I was basing it on the chapters of his course, but I decided to submit it in the hope that my personal perspective would serve to show how his course had changed my life. I printed everything, and went to deliver the papers. I approached him, very shyly, handed him all the pages, and said I'd like him to read them; it wasn't anything important, but it was a result of what his course had given me: courage to speak, courage to be a "person." He thanked me very seriously, and I went to another area of ​​the party. At the end, he came close to me and told me to go to Sunday mass at the end of the week so we could talk. I didn't like the idea because I liked him, and that was very wrong in my head... but I went. ... I stayed outside the church while he celebrated mass. I hated seeing him in those "chasuble"... I felt too dirty to enter the church. At the end, he waved to me, inviting me to come in and go to the altar where he was. When I got close, he sat in a chair to the side of the altar, and I sat next to him. He said he had read everything and was very impressed with my life story. He thanked me for trust him. And that he felt very sorry for my suffering. He paused, took a piece of paper from his chasuble pocket, took my hand, placed the paper on it, and said it was his phone number. He meant I shouldn't be scared, but he wanted to get to know me... as a man - not a priest. And if he could ask me out. If I accepted, I should call him, and we would make arrangements to go out, and talk. I was in shock and didn't know what to do. But I said YES. I couldn't believe that this wonderful man had noticed me. Many women weren't interested. Beautiful, light, perfect. But... he wanted me. Dirty, crooked, bruised, violated... He was the only person who truly understood me. He didn't judge me. And when I got angry about some silly talk, he would give a light laugh that would melt me! :D He was amazed by what I had been through, while everyone else was throwing stones at me!
